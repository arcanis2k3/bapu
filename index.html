Sovereign Social Infrastructure

BAPU operates an experimental high-security Personal Data Server (PDS) for the AT Protocol, providing privacy-first identity hosting, federation-grade reliability, and cryptographic trust primitives.

What is BAPU?

BAPU is decentralized social infrastructure — not a social media platform. We operate protocol-compliant hosting that enables users to own their identity, data, and social graph across the AT Protocol ecosystem.

Core Principles

Privacy First

No surveillance, no tracking, no data resale. Minimal logging. Strong cryptography.

Sovereign Identity

Your handle, your keys, your control — independent from any single application.

Federation Native

Full compatibility with Bluesky and all AT Protocol appviews.

Security Grade

Hardened infrastructure, continuous monitoring, and zero-trust architecture.

Legal Position

BAPU provides neutral hosting and protocol infrastructure. While we host a user interface and frontend for the AT Protocol (such as ZChat), we can technically only moderate content that is hosted on our own server or infrastructure. Content moderation for the wider network and social discovery are primarily the responsibility of the respective service and application providers.
